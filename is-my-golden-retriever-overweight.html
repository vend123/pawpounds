Is my Golden Retriever overweight?

Is my Golden Retriever overweight?

Golden Retrievers are food-motivated, eager to please, and wrapped in a thick double coat that hides weight beautifully — a combination that puts them near the top of the overweight-breed lists. Many owners genuinely can't see the extra pounds under all that fur.

Golden Retriever ideal weight

Healthy adult Golden Retriever weight (12+ months)
SexIdeal range
Male29–34 kg (65–75 lb)
Female25–32 kg (55–70 lb)

The coat hides everything. A Golden's dense double coat can disguise several kilos of weight gain, so the eye test fails on this breed — you have to go by feel. Weight also matters more here than for many breeds: Goldens are prone to hip and elbow issues and certain cancers, and excess weight worsens joint disease and inflammation. Keeping a Golden lean is one of the simplest things you can do for its long-term health.

How to tell if your Golden is overweight

  • Feel, don't look. Press gently through the coat — you should find ribs under a thin layer without digging.
  • Waist from above. Part the fur or press in behind the ribs; there should be a visible narrowing.
  • Side profile. The belly should tuck up behind the ribcage rather than hang level.

What to do about it

  • Weigh meals and keep treats under 10% of daily calories.
  • Use their energy. Goldens love swimming and fetch — both burn calories while sparing joints.
  • Lose slowly: about 1–2% body weight per week, rechecked monthly with your vet.

Frequently asked

How much should a Golden Retriever weigh?

Healthy adult males sit around 29–34 kg (65–75 lb) and females 25–32 kg (55–70 lb). Judge by feel as well, since the thick coat hides weight.

Why can't I tell if my Golden is overweight?

Their dense double coat disguises weight gain, so the eye test is unreliable. Feel for the ribs through the coat and check for a waist rather than judging by appearance.

Does weight really matter for Golden Retrievers?

Yes — more than for many breeds. Goldens are prone to hip and elbow problems and certain cancers, and excess weight worsens joint disease and inflammation. A lean Golden tends to live healthier for longer.

How do I help my Golden lose weight?

Measure meals, cut treats, lean on joint-friendly exercise like swimming and fetch, and aim for about 1–2% body-weight loss per week with your vet's guidance.
