Is my cat overweight?

Is my cat overweight?

Cats are quietly the bigger obesity problem: more than half of pet cats in surveys are overweight, and indoor cats especially. The tricky part is that a cat's healthy weight depends heavily on breed and frame — a lean Maine Coon can outweigh a chubby domestic shorthair — so shape beats the scale.

Ideal cat weight

Rough healthy weight by cat type
TypeTypical healthy weight
Average domestic cat3.6–4.5 kg (8–10 lb)
Small breeds (e.g. Siamese)2.3–4.5 kg (5–10 lb)
Large breeds (e.g. Maine Coon)5.5–9 kg (12–20 lb)

Because ranges overlap so much, the body checks below tell you more than any single number.

The belly-flap myth. That loose swinging pouch of skin on your cat's lower belly is usually the primordial pouch — a normal anatomical feature that protects the belly and lets cats stretch and run. It is not a sign of being overweight, and most healthy cats have one. Judge weight by the ribs and waist, not by the pouch. Plenty of owners put a perfectly lean cat on a diet because of it.

How to tell if your cat is overweight

  • Feel for ribs. Gently run your hands over the sides — ribs should be findable under a thin layer without pressing.
  • Look from above. There should be a slight waist behind the ribs. A rounded, oval outline that bulges outward means excess weight.
  • Side view. The belly should tuck up slightly — but remember the primordial pouch hangs low and is normal.

Why it matters for cats

Overweight cats face a sharply higher risk of feline diabetes, urinary problems, arthritis and fatty liver disease. Even a lean-looking loss of a few hundred grams can meaningfully cut those risks.

What to do about it

  • Measure meals and move away from free-feeding a full bowl all day.
  • Feed more protein, fewer carbs — cats are obligate carnivores; a vet can suggest a weight-management food.
  • Make them work for food with puzzle feeders and short play sessions.
  • Go slow. Cats must lose weight gradually — rapid loss can trigger dangerous fatty liver disease, so always involve your vet.

Frequently asked

How much should my cat weigh?

An average domestic cat is healthy around 3.6–4.5 kg (8–10 lb), but breed matters a lot — a Maine Coon may healthily weigh 5.5–9 kg. Use the rib and waist checks rather than the number alone.

Is my cat's hanging belly a sign it's fat?

Usually not. That low, swinging flap is the primordial pouch, a normal feature most healthy cats have. Judge weight by feeling for ribs and checking for a waist from above, not by the pouch.

Is my cat overweight or just fluffy?

Fur can hide a lot. Feel through the coat: if you can find the ribs easily under a thin layer and there's a slight waist, it's likely just fluff. If ribs are hard to find and the body is oval, it's weight.

How do I help my cat lose weight safely?

Measure meals, shift toward higher-protein food, add play and puzzle feeders, and lose weight slowly with your vet — cats that drop weight too fast risk fatty liver disease.
