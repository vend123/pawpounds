Is my cat overweight?

Cats are quietly the bigger obesity problem — and the swinging belly flap everyone worries about usually isn't fat at all.

More than half of pet cats in surveys are overweight, indoor cats especially. Healthy weight depends heavily on breed and frame, so shape beats the scale.

Ideal weight

Rough healthy weight by cat type
TypeTypical healthy weight
Average domestic cat3.6–4.5 kg (8–10 lb)
Small breeds (e.g. Siamese)2.3–4.5 kg (5–10 lb)
Large breeds (e.g. Maine Coon)5.5–9 kg (12–20 lb)

The belly-flap myth. That loose swinging pouch on a cat's lower belly is usually the primordial pouch — a normal feature that protects the belly and aids stretching. It is not a sign of being overweight, and most healthy cats have one. Judge weight by the ribs and waist, not the pouch.

How to tell if your pet is overweight

  • Feel for the ribs. Lay both hands flat on the ribcage; you should feel ribs easily under a thin layer without pressing.
  • Look from above. There should be a clear waist tucking in behind the ribs; a straight or bulging outline means no waist.
  • Check the side profile. The belly should tuck up behind the ribcage rather than hang level.

What to do about it

  • Weigh the food, don't scoop it. Eyeballing portions is the most common cause of overfeeding.
  • Count treats as calories — keep them under 10% of daily intake; swap biscuits for carrot or green bean.
  • Build up daily activity gradually, switching to low-impact options like swimming if joints are sore.
  • Go slow with cats. Rapid weight loss can trigger dangerous fatty liver disease — always involve your vet.
  • Reweigh monthly. Safe loss is about 1–2% of body weight per week; involve your vet for an overweight pet.

Frequently asked

How much should my cat weigh?

An average domestic cat is healthy around 3.6–4.5 kg (8–10 lb), but breed matters a lot.

Is my cat's hanging belly fat?

Usually not — it's the normal primordial pouch. Judge by feeling for ribs and a waist.

Is my cat overweight or just fluffy?

Feel through the coat: easy-to-find ribs and a slight waist mean fluff; buried ribs mean weight.
