Is my German Shepherd overweight?

Is my German Shepherd overweight?

German Shepherds are large, athletic working dogs, so a bit of extra weight can hide in that powerful frame. But this is a breed already prone to hip and elbow dysplasia, and every extra kilo accelerates joint wear — keeping a Shepherd lean is one of the most protective things you can do for its mobility.

German Shepherd ideal weight

Healthy adult German Shepherd weight (12+ months)
SexIdeal range
Male30–40 kg (66–88 lb)
Female22–32 kg (49–71 lb)

Weight and the Shepherd's hips. German Shepherds are genetically predisposed to hip and elbow dysplasia and arthritis. Excess weight loads those vulnerable joints and speeds up degeneration, often turning a manageable condition into a painful, mobility-limiting one years earlier than it needed to be. For this breed, lean isn't cosmetic — it's joint protection.

How to tell if your German Shepherd is overweight

  • Feel for ribs under a thin layer — findable without pressing through the coat.
  • Waist from above: a clear narrowing behind the ribs.
  • Side profile: a visible upward tuck of the belly behind the ribcage.

What to do about it

  • Measure meals and keep treats under 10% of daily calories.
  • Exercise consistently — this breed needs both physical and mental work — but protect sore joints with low-impact options if needed.
  • Lose weight slowly, about 1–2% per week, and involve your vet, especially if joints are already stiff.

Frequently asked

How much should a German Shepherd weigh?

Healthy adult males are around 30–40 kg (66–88 lb) and females 22–32 kg (49–71 lb). Confirm with body-shape checks, since the large frame hides weight.

Why does weight matter so much for German Shepherds?

They're genetically prone to hip and elbow dysplasia and arthritis. Excess weight loads those vulnerable joints and speeds degeneration, so keeping a Shepherd lean directly protects its long-term mobility.

Is my German Shepherd overweight or just big?

Feel for ribs and look for a waist from above. A large frame is normal for the breed, but buried ribs and a lost waist mean weight rather than size.

How do I help my German Shepherd lose weight?

Measure meals, cut treats, keep up consistent exercise (switching to low-impact activity if joints are sore), and aim for about 1–2% body-weight loss per week with your vet.
