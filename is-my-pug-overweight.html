Is my Pug overweight?

Is my Pug overweight?

Pugs are one of the most obesity-prone breeds there is: small, food-obsessed, low-energy, and built with a flat face that makes exercise harder. The weight creeps on easily — and on a Pug, it's genuinely dangerous rather than just unflattering.

Pug ideal weight

Healthy adult Pug weight
SexIdeal range
Male & Female6.3–8.2 kg (14–18 lb)

Why weight is dangerous for a Pug. Pugs are brachycephalic — extremely flat-faced, with very restricted airways. Extra fat around the neck and chest makes an already compromised airway worse, so overweight Pugs struggle to breathe, tire fast, and overheat dangerously (they can't pant efficiently to cool down). A few hundred grams matters more on a Pug than on almost any other dog. Vets are strict about Pug weight for exactly this reason.

How to tell if your Pug is overweight

  • Feel for ribs. They should be findable under a thin layer without pressing hard.
  • Look for a waist. Even on a compact dog there should be a slight tuck behind the ribs, not a sausage shape.
  • Watch breathing and heat tolerance. More snorting, quicker tiring, or struggling in warm weather can all be worsened by extra weight.

What to do about it

  • Measure every meal and be ruthless about treats — a Pug will convince you it's starving.
  • Exercise gently and only when cool. Short, calm walks in the morning or evening; never in heat.
  • Lose weight slowly with your vet, especially if breathing is already noisy.

Frequently asked

How much should a Pug weigh?

The breed standard puts a healthy adult Pug at 6.3–8.2 kg (14–18 lb). Confirm with the rib and waist checks, since Pugs hide weight in their compact build.

Why is being overweight so dangerous for Pugs?

Pugs are extremely flat-faced with restricted airways. Extra fat worsens breathing and makes overheating dangerous because they can't pant efficiently. Even a small amount of excess weight has an outsized effect.

My Pug always acts hungry — should I feed more?

No. Pugs are famously food-driven and will beg regardless of need. Measure meals by weight, keep treats minimal, and don't let the begging set the portions.

How do I help my Pug lose weight safely?

Measure food, cut treats, walk gently in cool parts of the day, and lose weight slowly under your vet's guidance — especially important if your Pug already breathes noisily.
