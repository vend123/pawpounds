Is my Rottweiler overweight?

A Rottweiler's muscle can hide fat — and on a breed prone to joint trouble, the extra load matters.

Rottweilers are large, powerful working dogs. Their muscular build can mask weight gain, and without enough exercise they gain readily — which strains joints already prone to problems.

Ideal weight

Healthy adult Rottweiler weight
SexIdeal range
Male43–61 kg (95–135 lb)
Female36–48 kg (80–100 lb)

Muscle can hide the fat. A Rottweiler's bulk makes the eye test unreliable — go by feel. This breed is prone to hip and elbow dysplasia and cruciate ligament injuries, and every extra kilo accelerates joint wear on an already heavy frame. A working Rottie needs a job as much as a diet.

How to tell if your pet is overweight

  • Feel for the ribs. Lay both hands flat on the ribcage; you should feel ribs easily under a thin layer without pressing.
  • Look from above. There should be a clear waist tucking in behind the ribs; a straight or bulging outline means no waist.
  • Check the side profile. The belly should tuck up behind the ribcage rather than hang level.

What to do about it

  • Weigh the food, don't scoop it. Eyeballing portions is the most common cause of overfeeding.
  • Count treats as calories — keep them under 10% of daily intake; swap biscuits for carrot or green bean.
  • Give real exercise and a job. This breed needs both physical and mental work to stay lean and content.
  • Reweigh monthly. Safe loss is about 1–2% of body weight per week; involve your vet for an overweight pet.

Frequently asked

How much should a Rottweiler weigh?

Males 43–61 kg (95–135 lb), females 36–48 kg (80–100 lb).

Is my Rottweiler muscular or fat?

Feel for ribs through the muscle; buried ribs and no waist mean weight rather than build.

Why does weight matter for Rotties?

They're prone to hip, elbow and cruciate problems that excess weight accelerates.
