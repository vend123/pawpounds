Dog body condition score chart

Vets judge weight by body condition score, not the scale — here's the 1–9 chart and how to score your own dog at home.

Vets don't judge a dog's weight by the number on the scale — they use the Body Condition Score (BCS), a hands-on assessment of how much fat a dog is carrying. Most vets use a 1–9 scale, where 4–5 is ideal. You can score your own dog at home in under a minute.

The 1–9 body condition scale

Dog body condition score (1–9)
ScoreWhat it means
1–3Underweight — ribs, spine and hip bones visible with no fat cover; obvious waist and tuck
4–5Ideal — ribs easily felt with a thin fat cover; visible waist from above; belly tucks up from the side
6–7Overweight — ribs hard to feel under fat; waist fading; little or no tuck
8–9Obese — ribs cannot be felt; no waist; belly rounded or sagging; fat deposits over spine and base of tail

How to score your dog in 3 checks

  • Ribs: run flat hands along the ribcage. Easy to feel under a thin layer = ideal. Have to press = too much fat.
  • Waist (from above): look down while your dog stands. A clear narrowing behind the ribs = ideal. Straight or bulging = overweight.
  • Tuck (from the side): the belly should rise up behind the ribcage. Level or sagging = overweight.

If two or three checks point to "overweight", your dog is likely a BCS 6–7 or higher. The good news is that BCS responds quickly to modest changes in food and activity.

Frequently asked

What is a dog body condition score?

It's a vet's hands-on rating of how much fat a dog carries, usually on a 1–9 scale where 4–5 is ideal — more reliable than the scale alone because it accounts for frame and breed.

What BCS should my dog be?

Ideal is 4–5 out of 9: ribs easily felt under a thin layer, a visible waist from above, and a belly that tucks up from the side.

How do I check my dog's body condition at home?

Do three checks — feel the ribs, look for a waist from above, and check the belly tucks up from the side. Two or three pointing to 'overweight' means a high BCS.

Is BCS better than weighing?

They work together. The scale tracks change over time; BCS tells you whether the current weight is actually healthy for that individual dog's frame.
